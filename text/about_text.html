About THRIVEair

THRIVEair was a community-based air monitoring project in South and Southwest Philadelphia. The study was co-designed as a partnership between Philly Thrive and researchers at the Drexel Dornsife School of Public Health and Lewis Clark State college. Data were collected from summer 2023 - summer 2024.

For more information on the study, visit our website at: THRIVEairPhilly.com

How to Use this Dashboard

Use the tabs on the left to explore the results from THRIVEair. You can download the raw data at the Data Table tab. Please note that you must request permission to re-use or publish this data.
You can request data re-use by emailing us at THRIVEair@gmail.com.

Interpreting These Data

Volatile organic compounds (VOCs) were monitored in one-week sessions, with begin dates representing the date of monitor deployment and end dates representing the date of data retrieval. Concentrations shown in these results are one week averages over the sampling period.

For example, a benzene concentration of 1 µg/m³ on 6/14/23 is interpreted as the one-week average concentration at that site for the week beginning on 6/7 and ending on 6/14.

We sampled at 9 sites weekly throughout the year, and at an additional 11 rotating community sites for 4 total weeks each, two weeks in summer and two weeks in winter. Note that the dashboard and interactive graphs only include measurements from sites we measured weekly. This is because measurements at the rotating community sites are only comparable to other sites that were measured during the same week--this requires a slightly more complex analysis that we are publishing in a forthcoming publication. Data for rotating community sites are still downloadable in the Data Table tab.

For more details on sampling methodology and interpretation, download the codebook available in the Data Table tab.
